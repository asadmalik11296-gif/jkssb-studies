1. 📜 Indian Constitution

📌 Basic Facts

Adopted: 26 January 1950

Drafted by: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (Chairman of Drafting Committee)

Total Articles: 395 (originally), now 470+

Total Schedules: 12

Preamble: Defines the objectives of the Constitution

🎯 Key Constitutional Bodies

Three Pillars of Democracy:

1. Executive: President, Vice-President, Prime Minister, Council of Ministers

2. Legislative: Parliament (Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha)

3. Judiciary: Supreme Court, High Courts, District Courts

🎯 Fundamental Rights (Articles 12-35)

1. Right to Equality - No discrimination

2. Right to Freedom - Speech, expression, assembly

3. Right against Exploitation - No forced labor, child labor

4. Right to Freedom of Religion - Freedom to practice religion

5. Cultural and Educational Rights - Protect minority interests

6. Right to Constitutional Remedies - Approach courts

🎯 Directive Principles of State Policy

Guidelines for Government:

• Provide adequate means of livelihood

• Reduce inequality in income and wealth

• Provide free and compulsory education

• Protect health and strength of workers
